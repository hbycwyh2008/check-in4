# Video Prototype Script

## Purpose

This is a 4-5 minute English script for the video prototype of the final AP CSA FRQ feedback redesign. You can record your screen while clicking through the live demo and read this script naturally. It does not need to be read word-for-word, but it should help you cover the full required walkthrough.

## Script

Hello, this video demonstrates my final prototype for redesigning AP Classroom feedback for AP Computer Science A free-response questions.

The goal of this design is to help students do three things more effectively after completing an FRQ practice:

1. see where they lost points,
2. understand why they lost points, and
3. know what to do next to improve.

In the current feedback experience, students may see their score, but they often still do not know which part of their answer caused the deduction, why that part was incomplete, or how to practice the same skill next time. My redesign tries to make feedback more actionable by connecting explanation with next-step practice.

Now I will walk through the prototype.

First, this is the overview page. Here, the student can immediately see the overall score and enter the detailed feedback flow. I kept this screen simple so the student first understands the general result before moving into a more detailed explanation. The purpose of this overview is not to explain everything at once, but to provide a clear starting point and reduce the chance that students feel overwhelmed by too much information at the beginning.

Next, the student moves into the rubric breakdown. This part helps the student identify where points were lost. Instead of only showing a score, the design breaks the result into rubric-related parts so the student can see which item was missed. This supports one of the main learning needs from my earlier research, because students need a clearer connection between deductions and the structure of the rubric.

After that, the prototype moves into the evidence and detail view. This is one of the most important parts of the design. Here, the feedback is tied more directly to the student’s own response. The idea is to reduce ambiguity by showing what was missing or incorrect in a more specific and localized way. In earlier feedback and prototype evaluations, participants consistently preferred feedback that was anchored to their own work instead of generic comments. So this part of the final design focuses on helping students understand not just that they lost points, but exactly why they lost points.

The next step is the practice-oriented part of the prototype. Once the student understands the problem, the system does not stop there. It helps translate that explanation into action. This section tells the student what skill needs more work and what they should practice next. I included this because one of the recurring problems in the original feedback experience is that students may understand a deduction after reading comments, but still do not know how to use that information for future improvement.

As the student continues through the flow, the prototype provides a short next-step practice path. The goal is to make improvement feel manageable. Instead of turning feedback into a long or abstract study plan, the design gives a more focused, immediate direction. This helps students move from understanding to action, which was one of the central goals of the redesign.

Overall, the prototype combines two major ideas from my earlier iterations. The first is anchored feedback, which helps students connect comments to specific parts of their response. The second is action-oriented guidance, which helps students know what to practice next. Earlier low-fidelity evaluations suggested that students valued both clearer explanation and more explicit next steps, so the final design combines those strengths into one medium-fidelity prototype.

This prototype is intended to reduce students’ dependence on extra teacher explanation by making the feedback page itself more useful as a learning tool. Rather than functioning only as a score report, the redesigned interface is intended to support reflection, diagnosis, and improvement.

Thank you for watching this video walkthrough of my final prototype.
