# 📋 Final evaluation — Interview & think-aloud

**Time:** 10–15 min (up to ~20 min with full think-aloud) · **Matches:** `Final_Evaluation_Survey.md`

---

## ✅ Session setup (facilitator — do not read aloud)

- [ ] Live demo URL ready (and/or video link)
- [ ] Recording consent script mentally rehearsed
- [ ] Spreadsheet or note template open for column headers (see bottom)
- [ ] Timer optional (2–3 min for think-aloud)

---

## 📌 Session flow (one glance)

| Step | Topic | ~Time |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 0 | Setup + consent | 1–2 min |
| 1 | Background (survey Q1–2) | 2 min |
| 2 | Task / think-aloud OR retrospective | 5–7 min |
| 3 | Verbal 1–7 items (survey Q3–9) | 3–5 min |
| 4 | Open-ended (survey Q10–13) | 2–3 min |
| 5 | Post confidence (survey Q14) | 1 min |
| 6 | Close | 30 sec |

---

## 🎯 When to use which mode

- Participant first **watches ~5 min video** OR **uses live demo ~5 min** (match your course plan).
- **Retrospective:** questions only after they finish.
- **Think-aloud:** they **share screen** and talk while clicking (semi-structured).

---

## 📢 0 · Opening (~1–2 min) — facilitator script

**Do:**

1. Thank them; say this is a **course project**, **anonymous**, for improving a design.
2. Ask permission to **record audio** / **session**; if no → notes only.
3. Send **demo** and/or **video** link.

**Read aloud (optional, verbatim OK):**

> 💬 I’ll ask you to look at a student-side feedback prototype for AP-style practice. There are no right or wrong answers—I’m testing the design, not you.

---

## 1️⃣ Section 1 — Background (survey items 1–2)

## AP experience (survey item 1)

**Ask:**

> 💬 Are you currently taking AP courses, or have you taken them before? Which subjects?

## Confidence with feedback pages (survey item 2)

Record answer as: `pre_feedback_confidence`

**Ask:**

> 💬 When you read assignment, quiz, or progress-check feedback pages, how well do you usually understand them?

> 💬 Please rate from **1** to **7**: **1** = not confident at all, **7** = very confident.

(You record the number.)

---

## 2️⃣ Section 2 — Task + observation (~5–7 min)

**Pick ONE path below.**

## Option A — Light think-aloud (recommended)

**Facilitator:** Ask them to **share screen** and open the prototype.

**Read aloud:**

> 💬 For the next 2–3 minutes, walk through overview → rubric breakdown → Evidence if you need it → Practice. Think aloud: what you’re looking for, where you hesitate, what feels clear.

**You jot keywords (don’t interrupt):**

- Do they surface **why points were lost**, **link to their answer**, **what to practice next**?
- Hesitation from **explanation** to **practice / queue**?

---

## Option B — Retrospective (after they watched the video)

**Ask in order:**

> 💬 If this were your score page, where would you click first? Why?

> 💬 Where does the interface tell you why you lost points?

> 💬 What do you think the practice / queue section is asking you to do?

---

## 3️⃣ Section 3 — Verbal 1–7 (survey items 3–9)

**Facilitator:** Read each line; they answer **1–7**. Optional follow-up: “Why that number?”

| Field | Read this prompt aloud |
| --- | --- |
| `q1_understand_why` | I can understand why points were lost. |
| `q2_identify_where` | I can identify where in my response the issue occurred. |
| `q3_know_what_to_practice` | I know what to practice next after seeing this feedback. |
| `q4_explanation_to_action_clear` | The transition from explanation to action is clear. |
| `q5_independent_improvement` | This prototype would help me improve more independently. |
| `q6_use_again` | I would use this kind of feedback page again. |
| `q7_overall_useful` | Overall, this feedback experience is useful for improvement after receiving AP-style feedback. |

**If short on time:** ask **q1, q3, q4, q7** only; cover gaps in Section 4.

---

## 4️⃣ Section 4 — Open-ended (survey items 10–13)

| # | Ask | Field |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | What was the **most useful** part, and **why**? | `useful_part` |
| 2 | What was **confusing or less useful**? | `confusing_part` |
| 3 | What feels **missing**? | `missing_part` |
| 4 | If you could change **one thing**, what would it be? | `one_change` |

---

## 5️⃣ Section 5 — Improvement confidence (survey item 14)

**Ask:**

> 💬 After seeing this prototype, how confident are you that you would know how to improve next time on a similar assignment? Again, 1 to 7.

Record → `post_improvement_confidence`

---

## 6️⃣ Closing (~30 sec)

- Thank them.
- Optional: contact for follow-up **only if** your course ethics allow.

---

## 📝 After the session — facilitator only (do not read aloud)

**One spreadsheet row per interview:**

`participant_id, method_interview, date, ap_experience, pre_feedback_confidence, q1..q7, post_improvement_confidence, useful_part, confusing_part, missing_part, one_change, thinkaloud_notes`

**Thematic codes (for your write-up):**

- clarity of explanations
- anchored evidence
- practice / next steps
- navigation & terminology
- missing information
- motivation & willingness to use again

---

## 📎 Writing tip — mix with a small survey (e.g. n = 3)

- **Results:** subsections **Survey (n = …)** and **Interview (n = …)**.
- **Limitations:** small sample, formative, no generalization to all AP students.
- **Discussion:** do survey + interview **point the same way** (e.g. explanation → practice)?
